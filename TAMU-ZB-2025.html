Explore DBR

Take a look at what DBR Designers do on a daily basis:

Mechanical
AC Systems (by building type)

System choices vary for offices, healthcare, and K-12: ventilation rates, redundancy, filtration, and energy recovery.

Sizing: Equipment, Ducts & Piping

Loads drive equipment selection; duct/pipe sizing balances flow, pressure drop, noise, and space constraints.

Heating/Cooling Load Basics

Envelope, occupancy, equipment, and schedules determine coil capacity and plant tonnage/BTUh.

Electrical
Power Path: Utility → Equipment

Service gear to panels and feeders, then branch circuits for lighting, receptacles, HVAC, and critical systems.

Demand & Diversity

Estimate building load from equipment lists and usage patterns; apply diversity to avoid oversizing.

Docs: Plans, Schedules, Risers

Mostly 2D documentation (lighting/power plans, schedules, risers) with selective 3D for coordination.

Plumbing
Sanitary & Vent

Wastewater collection and venting to protect traps and maintain flow to site utilities.

Domestic Water

Cold/hot/recirc distribution sized for pressure, flow, temperature maintenance, and comfort.

Stormwater

Roof drains, leaders, and building exits coordinated with civil to manage rainfall safely.

Technology
Communications

MDF/IDF rooms, racks, cabling pathways, WAP layouts, and AV devices for coverage and bandwidth.

Security

Access control, intrusion detection, and video coverage tuned to operations and sightlines.

Future-Ready Standards

Plan for change: spare pathways, power, cooling, rack space, and patching capacity.
